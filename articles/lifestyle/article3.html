3 Ways Reading Can Boost Your Mental Health

Sheyla Gyles 6:49 AM Lifestyle

https://dunbarconsultants.com/dunbar-news/the-importance-of-reading/

In the eighteenth century, author Joseph Addison and Sir Richard Steele wrote, “Reading is to the mind what exercise is to the body”---literally this is an honest quote. Books are fundamental for one's education but unexpectedly books are a beneficial necessity to one's mental health. Below listed are three compelling reasons why you should shut off your electronics and open a great book:


1.People who read often, become more empathetic

Getting lost in a good book and understanding the characters and their problems can make it easier for you to relate to the emotions and the feelings of others. The impact of reading is more imperative on those who read fiction books rather than non-fiction books.


2. Reading helps you destress

According to a research study by the University of Sussex, reading can reduce stress levels by 68%. Reading books, specifically reading books of the fiction genre, fully engages the mind and implements a creativity/imagination factor in the brain. In the same study by the University of Sussex, people who had read for six minutes exhibited slower heart rates, less muscle tension, and reduced stress levels. Dr. David Lewis, the neuroscientist who conducted the study, reported that reading, “is more than merely a distraction but an active engaging of the imagination as the words on the printed page stimulate your creativity and cause you to enter what is essentially an altered state of consciousness.” It turns out getting lost in a good book truly is the ultimate form of relaxation!


3.Reading Increases your ability to focus and intelligence level

Not only will reading help you become more relaxed and empathetic to others, but reading also creates you to become more intelligent by increasing your memory recall . Some think that book learning and memorizing is the only definition of intelligence. But really it is only one piece of the puzzle. It does engage the conscious mind and demands a lot of focus. The part of the brain that reading is most effective on is the prefrontal lobe. While you read it actually gets stronger hence, you are becoming more intelligent.
