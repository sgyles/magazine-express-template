Best Hiking Trails in the DMV

Kim Date Travel

You may want to get your daily workout done outside of your house, the perfect way to do so is by hiking. Although you can hike anytime you want, the best time to do so is during spring and fall. In the DMV, there are a lot of hiking trails that are perfect for beginners and some that are more challenging. There are many national parks with hiking trails within driving distance. Here are three of them:

Picture from: http://www.cparkre.com/homes-near-rock-creek-park.php%5C

Rock Creek Park (located in DC/MD) The Rock Creek Ramble is 3.1 mile loop hike that travels through the forest and along the creek. This trail is open to the public. It replaces the Boulder Bridge Hike when sections of the trail are closed.

https://www.nationalparks.org/explore-parks/catoctin-mountain-park

Catoctin Mountain Park (located in MD) There are two main trails at this park. The east side trails offer easy to hard hikes which can lead you to many overlooks and also gives you access to Cunningham Falls. The west side offers a more quieter experience. Hikers are most liking to come across wildlife and wetlands. Overall there is 25 miles of hiking for an adventurous person.

Link of picture

Old Rag Mountain (located in VA) Like any hike, you may want to plan ahead and prepare yourself. You will need at least 7 or 8 hours to complete this hike. This is a perfect hike to do on a weekday when it's less crowded, to better your experience there. Make sure you are wearing the appropriate shoes to complete this 9 mile hike. Hiking involves a lot of hard work but it is also fun. While hiking you can enjoy the things around you and find to love nature even more than you thought you would have. Try any of these DMV hikes that will challenge you!
