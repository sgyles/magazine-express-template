Stretches that will help improve football players performance

Wpfreeware 6:49 AM Technology

These are a list of stretches that will improve your performance on the field. Seated Groin and inner thigh stretch- These type of stretches are great for athletes it loosens up the hips, which makes it easier for you agility wise. How do this stretch: it down on the floor or ground with your legs in front of you. Reach and grab your right foot. It is okay to bend your knee to help your hand and foot connect. Gently pull your right foot up towards your groin bending until it is at a comfortable spot and the sole of the foot is facing your left thigh. Bend your left knee to bring your left foot toward your groin so that its sole touches the sole of your right foot. Hold your feet with your hands and rest your elbows on your knees. While keeping your back straight, allow your knees to fall towards the ground. You can apply gentle pressure on the inner thigh by pressing gently on the knees with the elbows. You should feel gentle pulling and tension in the groin. Runners Lunge- For football players doing Runner’s Lunges you will have a simple pose to help open your hips, relieve tension, and give your lower body a much needed stretch. This will definitely help performance on the field. How to do this stretch: According to paleohacks 1. Start in a tabletop position on your hands and knees. Step your right foot outside of your right hand. Heel-toe the foot forward and out a couple of inches so that your right ankle is kinda in front of your right knee. Keep your left knee down. 2.Keeping your hands planted inside of your right foot, press your hips forward to feel a stretch through the front of your hips. 3. Hold for 30 seconds and then switch sides. Standing calf stretch: This stretch stretches the foot and the ankle which helps athletes move their feet better without the high probability of injury to the foot or ankle. How to do this stretch: According to www. Stretching excersises.com “Stand away from a wall and put your right foot behind you and be sure your toes are facing forward. Lean forward at the ankle while bending the right knee and keeping your heel on the ground. Because the knee is flexed, tension is taken off the gastrocnemius and placed on the soleus. Hold this for 30 to 60 seconds. .

These stretches will help football player performance on the field as long as they follow these steps. .
